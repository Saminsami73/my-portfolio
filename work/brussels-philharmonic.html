Brussels Philharmonic

Creating a rich digital experience for a unique philharmonic orchestra.

Clean your trumpets and tune your violins.
Find out how I harmonized the online and offline experience.

Brussels Philharmonic is an orchestra with a long-standing history. Founded in 1935 by the Belgian national public broadcaster, the orchestra had the mission to introduce the public to the more serious repertoire.

Going to a Brussels Philharmonic concert is a refreshing experience. But their website didn’t project that image at all.I was brought in to translate the fresh and premium identity that Brussels Philharmonic has offline to an online environment and improve the overall visual experience to make the orchestra more appealing to a younger public.

What I did

UI Design | Developed by coding | UX design | Concept design

Great answers come from great questions.
Defining the briefing

During a first co-creative workshop with the client, we figured out what the actual expectations and requirements were.

Once I figured that out, I worked out with the boostrap and vanilla CSS to make the website look interactive. Both deliverables focused on the beating heart of the website: the calendar.

Setting the stage.
Page modelling & wireframing

With these broad outlines in mind, I started sketching components and UI patterns. I introduced a non-traditional navigation that’s sticky on the bottom of the screen. That directs the focus to the content, while keeping the menu available at all times.
